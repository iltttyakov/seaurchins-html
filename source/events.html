Events/News

  • Recent events have been unprecedented and caused a great deal of upheaval and distress to many families. In our region many families were impacted severely by the bushfires and now everyone has been impacted by the Coronavirus.

    We have created this page to provide some additional information. Our centre is constantly evaluating the risks of these events. Primarily here we would like to provide some further information about financial support as we know many families may be experiencing difficulties in these circumstances.

    The Australian Government is providing $5 million in Community Child Care Fund (CCCF) special circumstance grant funding to support child care services in bushfire affected areas, and there are some programs that support parents and families for example the Additional Child Care Subsidy. Families living in bushfire declared areas can get help with child care costs by applying for Additional Child Care Subsidy (temporary financial hardship). This is short-term help (up to 13 weeks) for families who use child care and are experiencing significant financial hardship.

    This subsidy is available to families who:

    • have experienced temporary financial hardship due to an event that happened in the last 6 months
    • have a substantially reduced ability to pay child care fees.

    Up to 100 hours of care if also available. For more information go to Services Australia’s Additional Child Care Subsidy page.

    Please do let us know if you are experiencing financial stress due to the bushfires or due to Coronavirus (COVID-19). We will endeavour to do our best to assist. You can contact us on info@seauchinschildcare.com.au
